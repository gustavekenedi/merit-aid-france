\begin{tablenotes}[para]
\item \textit{Notes:} This table reports estimates of the discontinuity in predicted outcomes at the merit aid eligibility threshold (16/20 Bac grade). Each predicted outcome is reported in the first column's rows, with the prediction regression adjusted R$^2$ in parenthesis. HS grad. stands for high school graduation. The predictors used for the predictions are: female dummy, age, high school graduation year, SES (5 categories), parent income, need-based grant echelon (4 cat.), education academie (Paris, 5 largest), born in Paris region dummy, high school track (3 categories) and private high school dummy (i.e., students' characteristics in Table \ref{tab:rd_observables}). The prediction model includes no interactions and is estimated by OLS. I report full sample estimates ('No Donut') and estimates obtained when excluding students with Bac grades in $[15.7, 16.05]$ ('Donut $[15.7, 16.05]$'). Moreover, estimates for two different bandwidths are reported: the MSE-optimal and $(15, 17)$ bandwidths. The MSE-optimal bandwidth, obtained using the \textit{rdrobust} R package, varies across each outcome. For MSE-optimal bandwidth estimates, the ranges in brackets correspond to associated robust 95\% confidence intervals, while they correspond to associated conventional 95\% confidence intervals for $(15, 17)$ bandwidth estimates. Statistical significance is computed based on the relevant p-value and ***, **, and * indicate significance at 1, 5, and 10\%, respectively.
